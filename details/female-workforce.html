Key Insights

Regional Variation

Nordic countries reach 70%+ participation through generous parental leave and childcare support. Middle East and North Africa average 21%, limited by cultural norms and legal restrictions.

Economic Impact

McKinsey estimates closing gender gaps in workforce participation could add $12 trillion to global GDP by 2025. Women's economic empowerment correlates with higher family incomes and better child outcomes.

Persistent Barriers

Unpaid caregiving (childcare, eldercare) disproportionately falls on women, consuming 3x more time than men globally. Lack of affordable childcare, parental leave, and workplace flexibility prevent full participation.

Changing Landscape

Remote work and gig economy offer flexibility but also precarity. STEM education initiatives aim to diversify traditionally male-dominated fields. Legal reforms in Saudi Arabia and other nations signal cultural shifts.
