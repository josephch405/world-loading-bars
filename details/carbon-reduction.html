Key Insights

Mixed Progress

While developed nations have reduced emissions by ~8% from 1990 levels, global emissions have increased by 60% due to economic growth in developing countries. The challenge is decoupling growth from emissions.

Regional Leaders

The EU has cut emissions by ~30% since 1990, UK by ~45%, while maintaining economic growth. Key strategies include renewable energy expansion, carbon pricing, and efficiency improvements.

Technology Drivers

Renewable energy costs have plummeted 90% for solar and 70% for wind since 2010. Electric vehicles, heat pumps, and green hydrogen are accelerating the transition.

The Path Forward

To meet Paris Agreement goals, global emissions must fall 45% by 2030 and reach net-zero by 2050. Current policies put us on track for 2.5-3°C warming.
