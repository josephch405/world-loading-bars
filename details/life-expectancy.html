Key Insights

Dramatic Gains

Global life expectancy has more than doubled since 1900, from ~32 to ~73 years. The 20th century saw the most rapid improvements in human history, driven by public health interventions and medical advances.

Regional Disparities

High-income countries average 80+ years (Japan: 84, Switzerland: 83), while low-income nations average ~64 years. Sub-Saharan Africa faces the largest gap, though narrowing rapidly.

Changing Mortality Patterns

In 1900, most deaths were from infectious diseases affecting children. Today, chronic diseases (heart disease, cancer, diabetes) in older adults dominate. The epidemiological transition reflects development success.

Future Outlook

Experts project continued gains from improved treatments for age-related diseases, better healthcare access in developing regions, and lifestyle interventions. Some optimists predict 90+ years globally by 2100.
