Key Insights

Remarkable Progress

Gender parity in primary education is one of development's greatest successes. In 1970, only 76 girls were enrolled for every 100 boys globally. Today, the ratio is 99:100, approaching perfect parity.

Secondary & Tertiary Gaps

While primary education reaches parity, gaps widen at higher levels. Secondary education averages 96% parity, and tertiary varies widely—many developed nations now enroll more women than men in university.

Remaining Challenges

Sub-Saharan Africa and South Asia still face barriers: poverty forcing girls into labor, early marriage, long distances to schools, lack of female teachers, and inadequate sanitation facilities.

Multiplier Effects

Educating girls yields enormous returns: higher lifetime earnings, lower child mortality, smaller families, better-educated next generation. UNESCO estimates each year of schooling increases a woman's earnings by 10-20%.
