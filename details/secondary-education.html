Key Insights

Rapid Growth

Secondary education completion has doubled globally since 1980, from ~30% to ~60%. This expansion reflects urbanization, economic development, and policy prioritization of education as a development driver.

Economic Necessity

In knowledge economies, secondary education is minimum for decent employment. OECD countries average 85%+ completion, while sub-Saharan Africa averages ~35%, creating widening global inequality in economic opportunity.

Persistent Barriers

Poverty drives students to work rather than study, early marriage affects girls, quality varies enormously (many complete without achieving competency), and rural areas lack schools and teachers.

Skills Gap Challenges

Even with rising completion rates, employers report skills mismatches. Future focus shifts to competency-based education, critical thinking, digital literacy, and lifelong learning rather than just credential completion.
